About Me

I am an integrative psychotherapist and counsellor offering both short-term and longer-term therapy. Being an integrative therapist means I draw on a range of different therapeutic approaches, rather than working from one single model. This allows me to tailor the work to each individual, their needs, and what feels most helpful at different stages of therapy. My work is relational at its core, meaning I place importance on the therapeutic relationship, as this is widely recognised as an important part of the healing process. I support clients in gently exploring how past experiences may still be showing up in their present—whether in relationships, emotions, self-esteem, or patterns of coping that no longer feel helpful. I specialise in relational trauma and childhood trauma, with a particular focus on attachment and early relational experiences. My work in this area is also informed by personal experience, which I hold in mind alongside my professional training and which deepens my understanding of the impact of early relational experiences and attachment trauma. My work also extends to supporting parents who are navigating the emotional pressures of parenting, including the desire to understand themselves more deeply in order to show up differently in their relationships with their children. I also have extensive experience working within mental health services, supporting people with a wide range of difficulties including anxiety, depression, stress, overwhelm, low self-worth, and feeling stuck or disconnected. I aim to offer a calm, non-judgemental space where you can feel heard, understood, and supported at your own pace. My intention is always to meet you where you are, rather than where you think you “should” be, and to work alongside you in building insight, self-compassion, and more supportive ways of relating to yourself and others. I hold a degree in Psychology with a counselling specialism, as well as an accredited Professional Diploma in Psychotherapeutic Counselling Practice. I am accredited by the National Counselling and Psychotherapy Society and work in line with their ethical framework. I offer therapy online via video and telephone, providing flexibility and accessibility from the comfort of your own space.

Fees

My standard fee is £60 per 50-minute session. I also offer a limited number of concessionary spaces at a reduced rate for those on lower incomes or who may otherwise find therapy financially inaccessible, including students, care leavers, and those receiving benefits. These spaces are limited and offered on a case-by-case basis. If you feel you may require a concessionary space, please feel welcome to mention this when enquiring and we can look at availability together.

Getting in touch

If you would like to enquire about starting therapy, please email me with a brief outline of what you are looking for support with. From there, I will get back to you to arrange a brief 15-minute introductory telephone call. This is an opportunity for us to have a short conversation, explore what you are looking for, and see whether working together feels like a good fit. There is no pressure in this call—it is simply a chance to get a sense of each other.

If we decide to go ahead, I will send over some initial paperwork, including a contract and intake forms for you to complete.

We will then arrange a full initial consultation session at the agreed rate. This is a 50-minute session where we can explore things in more depth and begin to think about how we might work together going forward.
